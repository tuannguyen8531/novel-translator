# Common Translation Rules (All Languages -> English)

## General Guidelines
- Translate naturally and fluently, as if the text was originally written in English
- Do NOT translate word-by-word; translate by meaning, intent, and scene context
- Preserve the original tone: formal scenes stay formal, casual scenes stay casual
- Preserve paragraph structure, scene breaks, and dialogue formatting
- Keep the reading experience smooth for English web novel readers

## Scene Analysis & Dynamic Filtering
- Before translating, infer the scene type and adjust the style accordingly
- Action scenes: prioritize momentum, short clauses, vivid verbs, and clear spatial flow
- Romance or drama scenes: preserve emotional subtext, hesitation, silence, and tension without overexplaining
- Comedy scenes: keep timing, contrast, and punchlines natural in English
- Info-dump or exposition scenes: use clear formal English, but avoid sounding like a textbook unless the source does
- Internal monologue: preserve the character's rhythm, bias, uncertainty, and emotional drift

## Dialogue
- Use natural English dialogue punctuation and paragraphing
- Use contractions when the speaker is casual and the setting allows it
- Avoid contractions for highly formal, archaic, ceremonial, or stiff characters unless the source tone is informal
- Match each speaker's age, status, personality, and relationship in diction
- Keep exclamations, ellipses (...), interruptions, and trailing thoughts when they carry emotion or timing
- Do not overuse dialogue tags; prefer beats and natural English flow when the speaker is clear

## Relationship, Address, and Voice
- Track social distance internally and reflect it through diction, titles, and formality
- Do not flatten all relationships into first-name address; preserve hierarchy when it matters
- Use titles such as Master, Teacher, Senior, Commander, Your Highness, Young Master, Lady, or Sir only when they fit the source context
- When a title sounds unnatural in English, translate the relationship instead of preserving a literal form
- Keep each character's reference style consistent across the chapter and with the glossary
- Avoid adding explicit pronouns or names where English can preserve ambiguity naturally

## Names & Proper Nouns
- Keep character names consistent throughout the entire novel
- Refer to the GLOSSARY for established translations or romanizations
- For new names not in the glossary, choose a readable English rendering or romanization
- Do not randomly alternate between translated names, romanized names, titles, and nicknames
- Preserve meaningful aliases, epithets, organization names, skill names, place names, and item names consistently

## Internet/Modern Slang
- Translate internet slang to natural English equivalents when possible
- Match register: gamer slang, forum slang, campus slang, corporate speech, and texting should not sound identical
- If no natural equivalent exists, keep the original only when it is a known term or context makes it understandable
- Avoid outdated English slang unless the source specifically sounds dated

## Anti-Translationese
- Avoid stiff calques, unnatural word order, and repeated sentence frames from the source language
- Prefer specific English verbs over generic verbs when they sharpen the scene
- Avoid overusing "suddenly", "actually", "just", "that", and "as expected" unless the source emphasis requires it
- Convert idioms by meaning; use a literal image only when the image itself matters
- Do not mechanically preserve every honorific, sentence ending, or discourse marker
- Avoid explaining cultural references inside the translation unless the source itself explains them

## Boldness & Sensory Writing
- When the scene is intense, allow shorter, broken sentences to create rhythm and tension
- Use concrete sensory verbs and images when they strengthen the scene
- Preserve physical sensation, atmosphere, and emotional pressure without adding new details
- For sensitive or adult content, prefer literary and context-appropriate language over clinical phrasing
- Keep violence, fear, embarrassment, and intimacy at the same intensity as the source

## Formatting
- Preserve line breaks between paragraphs
- Keep special formatting like【brackets】for in-story posts, messages, etc.
- Keep『brackets』for chat messages or system notifications
- Preserve system windows, status screens, forum posts, chat logs, and UI-like text as recognizable in-world formats
- Use English punctuation naturally, but keep source-specific brackets when they are part of the story's presentation

## Quality Standards
- No missing sentences or paragraphs — translate EVERYTHING
- No added content — do not invent details not in the source
- No translator notes, footnotes, or explanations in the output
- No markdown wrappers, commentary, or summaries in the output
- Preserve special symbols and formatting such as 【Posts】, 『Systems』, and 「Dialogue」
