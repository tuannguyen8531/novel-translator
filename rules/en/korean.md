# Korean -> English — Language-Specific Rules

## Names
- Keep Korean names romanized consistently unless the glossary specifies otherwise
- Use the same spacing and order for a character's name across the entire novel
- Do not convert Korean names to Chinese-style or Vietnamese-style readings
- Keep organization, guild, school, and company names consistent with the glossary

## Honorific System
- 존댓말 (formal speech): use polite, measured English; avoid overly casual contractions
- 반말 (casual speech): use casual, direct English
- ~님 (nim): express respect through title, Sir/Ma'am, Lord, Master, or the person's role when natural
- ~씨 (ssi): Mr. / Ms. / first-name address, depending on social distance
- ~야 / 아 (ya/a): direct name call, usually casual or intimate
- 선배 -> senior / sunbae, depending on setting and glossary
- 후배 -> junior / hoobae, depending on setting and glossary
- 형 / 오빠 / 누나 / 언니 -> older brother / oppa / noona / unnie only when the term matters; otherwise reflect closeness through tone
- 아저씨 / 아줌마 -> mister / ma'am / auntie / older man/woman, depending on tone

- 무림 -> Murim / martial world, depending on glossary

## Ranking, Corporate, and Modern Terms
- 랭킹 -> ranking
- 재벌 -> chaebol / conglomerate family, depending on context
- 회장 -> chairman
- 대표 -> CEO / representative / director, depending on company context
- 팀장 -> team leader / manager
- 선생님 -> teacher / doctor / sir/ma'am, depending on context

## Style Notes
- Korean web novels often move quickly; keep dialogue punchy and readable
- Preserve sentence fragments and clipped replies when they show tension or attitude
- Avoid literal repetition of sentence endings; convert them into natural English rhythm
- Use formality, distance, and title choice to preserve hierarchy instead of adding explanations
- Keep strong expressions (!, ...) when they carry emotional force

## Cultural and Register Notes
- Retain Korean kinship/address terms only when the novel's flavor depends on them or the glossary establishes them
- Otherwise, translate the relationship or social distance naturally
- Do not insert translator notes for honorifics, food, schools, military, guilds, or social hierarchy
- Preserve comedic deadpan, bluntness, and social awkwardness when present
